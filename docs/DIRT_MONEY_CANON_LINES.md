## Roy dealer pop-in events (system: sprint with salvage/flip loop)
- "Got something on the lot you might want to see."
- "Previous owner says it only smokes when it starts. Which is a sentence worth charging less for."
- "You looking to buy, or just hoping I talk myself down?"
- "I can hold it till Friday. After that it belongs to whoever brings a check."

Design notes (Director): dealer visits = opportunity, temptation, risk, relationship test.
Retail Roy offers different deals than Respect Roy.

## Daily event philosophy (system: weighted event scheduler, sprint 9)
Events come TO the player — visitor, phone call, storm warning, neighbor request, dealer offer,
bank notice, pest alert, crop stage update. Weighted by background, reputation, contracts,
weather, crop stage, equipment condition, debt, prior choices, county standing. Usually
conversations, not popups: even mechanical events get a human face.

## Location purposes (canonized in UI/WORLD specs; kept here as the Director's checklist)
Every location needs functional + social + event purpose. Diner: meet people, hear about jobs,
soft warnings, reputation reflected back — not just gossip. Co-op: board, prices, advice,
handshake reputation. Dealer: offers, service, trade-ins, reputation pricing. Bank: loans, credit
pressure, paperwork voice. Salvage: parts, mechanic jobs, equipment rumors, respect checks.
Farm: work orders, events arriving at your door.
