# DIRT MONEY — Phase 1 Playtest Script
*Internal. The slice is no longer trying to prove we can build systems. It is trying to prove
that three runs through the same county feel different enough to replay. That is the test.*

## Setup
Launch the game (Godot → F5, or `godot --path C:\dev\Dirt_Money`). The `[Playtest]` button on the
farm HUD shows ledger, flags, relationships, and offers instant restart / background switch /
summary export. Use it freely between runs — that's what it's for.

## The four runs

### Run 1 — Old School, honorably
Play the traditionalist straight: plant inside the windows (corn closes Day 6), shake on the corn
delivery and KEEP it, help Hollis when the storm comes, haul his hay, visit the diner. Try the
Read the Land option at the elevator after Day 8.

### Run 2 — Mechanic, chase the wrench
Fix the baler (take the check). Work the co-op repair pipeline it unlocks. When Gus flags you
down, inspect BOTH machines — read the wrench-eye lines carefully — buy the one you believe in,
restore it, sell it to Roy. Farm only hay. Machines are the business.

### Run 3 — IT Nephew, data against distrust
Watch the dashboard: sell on forecast peaks, hold when tomorrow pays better. Feel the early
game: labor costs, greenhorn mistakes, a county that starts cold, credit that may already be
tight. Try the Price Alerts option with Marge after Day 8. Argue a rate with Earl.

### Run 4 — Any background, burn it down
Shake on contracts and miss them on purpose. Decline the storm. Watch what happens: Patti's
lines, Marge's door, Earl's rate, the HUD's credit warning, your verdict. The county turning on
you should feel SOCIAL before it feels financial.

## After each run, answer in writing
1. Did the background feel meaningfully different — in what you SAW and DID, not just numbers?
2. Did the calendar matter? Could you feel Day 6, Friday deadlines, Day 8, Day 30 approaching?
3. Did downtime feel productive — did the middle of the month pull you into the county?
4. Did town visits have purpose, or were you wandering?
5. Did reputation feel social before financial?
6. Did the report card feel like a verdict?
7. Did anything feel fake, repetitive, or gamey? Name the exact moment.

## The kill metric
After finishing one run: did you actually want to start another background? If the answer is
"not really," write down what was missing from the itch. That answer is worth more than
everything else on this page.
