# Dirt Money v0.2 Playtest Feedback

Date: 2026-07-07

This pass summarizes the friend playtest feedback and tracks the first incremental engagement fixes. The goal is not a rewrite; it is to move Dirt Money toward a repeatable loop where cash, debt, reputation, contracts, weather, and equipment all matter.

## Critical Bugs

- Machine Shed `Repair on Credit` charged cash first and could cost more without explaining why.
- Repair button labels did not reliably describe the actual payment path.

## UX / Navigation Issues

- The old `Advance Week` action felt too plain and game-like.
- Field detail required backing out to switch fields.
- Weekly reports needed clearer “what happened” feedback.

## Economy / Balance Issues

- First harvest revenue could erase debt pressure too quickly.
- Planting, harvest, fertilizer, spray, and debt pressure were too forgiving.
- The season was too short for reputation, contracts, events, and progression to matter.

## Contract Issues

- Contracts could be accepted and completed immediately.
- The board felt static.
- Jobs lacked enough time, risk, requirements, opportunity cost, and reputation impact.

## Reputation Issues

- Reputation existed mostly as a number.
- It did not clearly affect bank terms, contract access, or visible opportunities.

## Event / Content Issues

- Weather and county events were too light.
- Players wanted memorable events: wet fields, drought, storm damage, bank pressure, neighbor requests.
- Reports needed to show events and consequences.

## Progression Issues

- Players needed meaningful long-term money sinks beyond paying debt and repairing equipment.
- Desired directions include land access, equipment upgrades, farm improvements, and operation expansion.

## Audio / Polish Issues

- The game had no sound support.
- A safe first step is a muted/volume setting and generated UI tones that only play after interaction.

## Future Expansion Ideas

- Multi-season play.
- Deeper equipment market.
- More land and lease negotiations.
- More NPC-specific relationship arcs.
- More crop insurance, pests, disease, frost, and flood systems.
- Better animation pacing controls for weekly reports.
